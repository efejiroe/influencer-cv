# Influencer Dynamics Measurement Framework

Here is a summary of the dynamics and metrics we are measuring, logically ordered from immediate launch impact (hype) to long-term algorithmic virality and audience quality. This structure is designed to help stakeholders understand how an influencer's audience behaves over time.

| Metric | Complete Definition | Potential Visualisation | Insight Interpretations | Commercial Applications |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Peak View Velocity ($V_{max}$)** | The maximum Views Per Hour (VPH) achieved usually in the first 0-12 hours of launch. | **Spike Chart / Line Graph:** A timeline showing VPH peaking sharply shortly after the upload timestamp. | Indicates the raw "Hype" and FOMO generated by the creator. Measures how many viewers have notifications turned on and watch immediately. | **Flash Sales & Product Drops:** Selecting creators who can guarantee immediate, massive traffic to a website on Day 1 of a campaign. |
| **2. Innovation Coefficient (Bass $p$)** | The baseline adoption rate driven by the creator's "Loyalist" followers independent of algorithmic push. | **Bar Chart / Gauge:** Comparing the $p$ value against the beauty industry benchmark (e.g., ~0.02 to 0.05). | High $p$ means the creator has a "Cult Following" with deep trust. These are viewers who actively seek out the creator's content. | **Core Brand Ambassadorships:** Best for high-trust campaigns, complex product tutorials, or shifting brand perception among a dedicated niche. |
| **3. Imitation Coefficient (Bass $q$)** | The viral adoption rate driven by the algorithm (Home Feed, Up Next) and social sharing. | **S-Curve (Cumulative Views):** A steep, sustained S-Curve indicates high imitation, while a flat curve indicates low imitation. | High $q$ indicates "Algorithmic Virality." The content spreads rapidly beyond the creator's subscriber base to new, undiscovered audiences. | **Top-of-Funnel Brand Awareness:** Ideal for expanding market share, reaching new demographics, and driving massive aggregate reach. |
| **4. Peak Saturation Point (Bass $M$)** | The mathematical ceiling or maximum potential reach of the video before the algorithm stops pushing it. | **Target vs. Actual Chart:** A bar comparing the video's current views against its projected $M$ ceiling. | Reveals the true "Market Size" for that specific content theme. Shows if a topic is niche (low $M$) or broadly appealing (high $M$). | **Campaign Forecasting & ROI:** Allows marketing directors to predict the final CPM (Cost Per Mille) of a sponsored video within 48 hours of it going live. |
| **5. Intermittent Revs (Instant Velocity Power)** | Sudden, localized spikes in `view_velocity_inst` after the initial 24-hour launch window. | **Pulse Graph:** Highlighting secondary, smaller spikes occurring days after the upload. | Indicates the video is being "resurrected" by the algorithm, shared by a larger account, or picking up SEO traffic, delaying the natural decay. | **Evergreen Content Selection:** Identifying creators whose content has a longer shelf life, providing continuing value weeks after a launch. |
| **6. Weighted Engagement Ratio** | Comments (weighted 2x) + Likes, divided by Total Views. | **Scatter Plot:** Plotting Engagement % on the Y-axis against Total Views on the X-axis. | High comment volume indicates deep viewer satisfaction and active discussion. A ratio > 4% strongly signals to the YouTube algorithm to push the video. | **Predicting Click-Through Rates (CTR):** High engagement correlates strongly with viewer intent. These creators are more likely to drive link clicks and affiliate sales. |

## Suggested Presentation Flow

When presenting this to a marketing director or agency stakeholder, I recommend framing it as a three-part story:

1.  **The Hook (Velocity & $p$):** "How fast can this creator get your product in front of buyers on launch day?"
2.  **The Scale ($q$ & $M$):** "How far will the YouTube algorithm push your brand to *new* customers who aren't even subscribed?"
3.  **The Action (Engagement & Revs):** "Will the audience actually care, comment, and ultimately click the link to buy?"
